In this paper, we propose a modular framework for temporally grounded VideoQA consisting of four components: a Refiner, a Retriever, a Validator, and a Generator. Our design is motivated by the observation that reliable grounded answer prediction depends not only on strong reasoning ability, but also on the quality of the temporal evidence provided to the final answer generator. To address this challenge, we first use the Refiner to rewrite answer candidates into grounding-friendly queries, then use the Retriever to localize candidate evidence spans, the Validator to score and rank the retrieved evidence, and the Generator to produce the final answer based on the highest-ranked clip.

Experimental results on NExT-GQA and ReXTime show that our framework improves both temporal grounding quality and grounded question answering accuracy. In particular, the gains on stricter grounding-aware metrics indicate that the proposed pipeline is effective at producing more precise and reliable evidence, rather than merely retrieving roughly relevant moments. Our analysis further suggests that answer-aware refinement, explicit evidence selection, and evidence-conditioned generation all play important roles in achieving robust reasoning across time.

Overall, our findings show that temporally grounded VideoQA benefits from explicitly separating evidence refinement, retrieval, validation, and generation. In future work, it would be interesting to extend the framework to multi-span reasoning, stronger query rewriting, and more challenging long-form video settings where evidence may be sparse, compositional, or temporally distant.
