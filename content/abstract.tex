Temporally grounded Video Question Answering requires a model not only to answer a question correctly but also to identify the temporal evidence that supports its prediction. This setting is particularly challenging for multiple-choice video reasoning, where raw answer candidates are often suboptimal for temporal localization, and correct prediction depends on both reliable evidence retrieval and grounded reasoning over the retrieved moments. In this paper, we present a modular framework for temporally grounded VideoQA consisting of four components: a \emph{Refiner}, a \emph{Retriever}, a \emph{Validator}, and a \emph{Generator}. The Refiner first rewrites answer candidates into grounding-friendly queries, enabling the Retriever to localize more relevant temporal evidence. The retrieved candidate moments are then scored and ranked by the Validator, and the final answer is produced by the Generator based on the highest-ranked evidence rather than the full video. To support this design, we adopt a role-specific strategy in which the Retriever and Validator are optimized for their designated functions, while the Generator serves as the final grounded reasoning module. Experiments on NExT-GQA and ReXTime show that our framework improves both temporal grounding quality and grounded question answering accuracy, with especially clear gains under strict grounding-aware evaluation. These results highlight the importance of combining answer-aware query refinement, stronger temporal retrieval, explicit evidence validation, and grounded answer generation for reliable video understanding.
