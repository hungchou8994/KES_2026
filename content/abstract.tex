Grounded Video Question Answering (VideoQA) requires models to both predict correct answers and localize the temporal evidence supporting them. While recent advances in multimodal video reasoning have improved these capabilities, most existing methods assume that answer candidates can be directly used as retrieval queries. This assumption is often violated in multiple-choice grounded VideoQA, where answer candidates are abstract, incomplete, or poorly aligned with observable events. To address this limitation, this paper proposes \emph{EviR-Vid}, an evidence-oriented framework that decomposes grounded VideoQA into query reformulation, temporal retrieval, evidence validation, and answer generation. EviR-Vid rewrites answer candidates into retrieval-friendly queries, retrieves candidate temporal spans, ranks them by evidence reliability, and predicts answers based on the most reliable evidence clip. Experiments on NExT-GQA and ReXTime demonstrate that EviR-Vid consistently improves performance under grounding-aware evaluation, achieving gains of 5.77 IoU@0.5 and 7.05 Acc@IoU=0.5 over existing state-of-the-art methods, while also achieving the best or comparable answer accuracy. These results highlight the importance of aligning answer formulation with temporal evidence retrieval for reliable grounded video reasoning.
